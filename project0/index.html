Part 1 — Selfie: Wrong Way vs Right Way

Goal: demonstrate framing/lighting choices and their impact on portrait quality.

These are portraits of my friend in CS180 who’s taking the class with me. In the first photo I’m a bit too close and the angle/light feel harsher, so it’s not super flattering. In the second photo I backed up to eye level with softer light and a cleaner wall which is more natural.

Part 2 — Architectural Perspective Compression

Goal: compare wide vs telephoto perspective and comment on “compression.”

I took these pictures while walking home: in the first picture I’m close with a wider view, so the background stretches away. In the second picture I stepped back and zoomed in, and everything looks more compressed.

Part 3 — The Dolly Zoom

Goal: keep subject size constant while changing focal length and camera distance.

I shot a quick dolly-zoom here using different objects in my apartment: I moved backward while zooming in to keep the subject about the same size. The background stretches and slides in, giving that cinematic, slightly trippy vibe.
